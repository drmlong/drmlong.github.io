About

I work in Experimental Pragmatics, an area of Cognitive Science that employs psycholinguistic techniques to investigate theories of how we produce and interpret language in context.

Currently I'm a visiting postdoctoral researcher at the MPI working on the RCN-funded project "Monitoring listeners' attention focus during referential communication". Prior to this I was a postdoc at Oslo Uni working on the RCN-funded project "Vision shapes language: referential communication in blind, sighted and newly-sighted individuals" and a one-year project "Linguistic and Cognitive Factors in Effective Referential Communication". My PhD thesis (at Edinburgh Uni) examined the cognitive mechanisms underlying perspective-taking over the lifespan and the impact of later-life language learning on cognitive ageing.
